Resources for the Less Fortunate in Winston-Salem


Within Winston-Salem there is an inherent issue in which not everyone has access to the resources they need. Sometimes these resources are very difficult for people to find, so I am putting them together within this website in hopes of getting these resources out to people who need them most.



City of Winston-Salem Official Homelessness Site

This website offers up some information about the Continuum of Care (CoC), which is an effort to end homelessness within the community. The goal of the Continuum of Care program is to plan, implement, and operate an optimized homeless crisis response system that assures homelessness is rare, brief, and non-recurring for people in our community.

You can call their number, 211, for more assistance with homelessness.



Bethesda Center

This website offers a variety of information, ranging from hours of shelter operation to when their next event is. Their day shelter offers restrooms and showers, laundry facilities, telephone access, a mailing address, and computer/internet access. In addition to everything the day time services offer, the night time services offer permanant housing, family reunification, case management and mental/physical health care.

Crisis Control Ministry

Crisis Control Ministry provides help with basic life needs to those facing a crisis in Forsyth and Stokes Counties. They work on a first come first serve basis. They provide short-term emergency services for residents of Forsyth and Stokes Counties including rent/mortgage payments, utility bill assistance; food from their Client Choice Food Pantry; and medications from their Licensed Free Pharmacy
